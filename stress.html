My story

At the beginning of this year I was quite hopeful and claimed that 2020 would finally be my "year", I even had my 2020 goals written out but was scarpped quickly. As we all know why... From that moment onwards I felt like I was stuck. The new normal was strange getting used to. I was stressed, scared, sleepless, you name it. For weeks I was down. I really thought what was the point in doing things. One day, I watched the sunrise and birds fly and that calmness really was soothing even when I was going through a hard time. I decided I should find a way to better myself and slowly started to study languages which I love doing but I hadn't studied for awhile. This was my way of overcoming and getting through. Now I feel much better. This year has been something I've learnt that it's okay to take time for myslef, not to rush, and whatever happens stressfull or not I will get through it. - Zarmeena

Stress relief blogs to visit

Headspace

Headspace is a blog and app that focuses on meditation. Research has shown that meditation programs may reduce emotional symptoms including anxiety, depression, and stress, and improve physical symptoms such as pain.

headspace

Run Wild Retreats + Wellness

Elinor Fish founded Run Wild Retreats + Wellness in 2010. The company offers running and wellness retreats in the United States, as well as in places such as Iceland, Spain, and Switzerland. The retreats aim to help runners reduce their stress so that they have more energy and vitality.

Run Wild Retreats + Wellness

The Journey Junkie

Allie Flavio created The Journey Junkie in 2014 as a place where yoga, travel, and life combine. The blog includes easy-to-follow yoga tutorials, yoga programs and challenges, and meditations. Following Allie’s videos – which are packed with poses and breathing techniques – and listening to her calming voice is a guaranteed stress-buster.
